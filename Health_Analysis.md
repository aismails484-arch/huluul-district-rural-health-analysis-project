# Public Health Data Analysis: Huluul District

## 📊 1. Disease Burden Analysis
The demographic of Huluul and the surrounding 18 villages (serving 1M+ population) shows a high prevalence of:
* **Chronic Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs):** Hypertension, Diabetes, and Thyroid disorders due to dietary changes and climate stress.
* **Neurological Emergencies:** High rates of stroke without a local rehabilitation or diagnostic center.
* **Trauma & Fractures:** Frequent bone injuries due to the rugged terrain and nomadic lifestyle, requiring specialized orthopedic intervention.

## 📈 2. Geographic Gap Analysis
* **Current Access:** Only 1 MCH and 1 Ambulance exist for the entire district.
* **Target:** Establishing a 250-bed referral hub reduces the travel distance from 150km to within a 35-50km radius for specialized surgery and diagnostics.

## 🔬 3. Technical Strategy
The integration of **1.5T MRI** and **128-Slice CT Scan** is critical for:
1. Early detection of Thyroid malignancies.
2. Precision mapping for Neurosurgery and Stroke management.
3. Advanced orthopedic visualization for complex fractures.
